Certify Genie: AI-Powered CA Certificate Generation from Audited
Financial Statements — Net Worth, Turnover, and Working Capital

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CATEGORY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

AI & CA Office Automation / Practice Efficiency

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. INTRODUCTION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Issuing a Net Worth or Turnover Certificate should be a five-minute job. In
practice, it rarely is. A CA receives a scanned balance sheet, manually reads
out the figures, opens a Word template, types in the numbers, prays nothing is
transposed, and then cross-checks the annexures against the certificate body
before printing. If the client needs a second copy with a different valuation
method, the cycle restarts. Certify Genie compresses that workflow to under
two minutes — the CA uploads the document, Claude AI extracts the figures,
the system validates the accounting equations, and a professional ICAI-format
certificate is generated in both PDF and DOCX. The entire pipeline runs PII
redaction before the document reaches the AI, keeping the firm compliant with
India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. PROBLEM STATEMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

I run a Virtual CFO practice in Chennai — ZenCFO — and we handle a high volume
of compliance certificates every month: Net Worth Certificates for bank credit
facilities, Turnover Certificates for government tender eligibility (GeM, CPWD,
state PWDs), Working Capital Certificates for NBFC borrowings, and the
occasional Net Worth Certificate needed for court proceedings or PE investor
submissions.

Every one of these follows the same pattern. Client sends a scanned PDF of
their audited balance sheet. The scan quality is average at best — sometimes
it is a photo taken on a phone. I open it alongside a Word template, manually
transcribe Share Capital, Reserves & Surplus, Accumulated Losses, Total Assets,
Total Liabilities. I check the arithmetic. I notice the reserves figure I typed
doesn't match what's in the annexure. I fix it. I generate the certificate. I
notice the certificate says Net Worth is Rs. 42,30,000 but the detailed workings
say Rs. 42,80,000. Somewhere a digit slipped.

Manual transcription isn't just slow. It is a reliability problem. The ICAI
Guidance Note on Certificates is clear: the CA is responsible for the accuracy
of the figures in the certificate. But the current workflow has no systemic
check — nothing flags when the final Net Worth in the certificate doesn't
reconcile to Share Capital plus Reserves minus Losses.

A second problem is the method question. Net Worth can be computed multiple
ways — Book Value, Tangible Net Worth (deducting intangibles), as defined under
Section 2(57) of the Companies Act 2013, as per SEBI LODR, NAV-based. The
right method depends on the entity type and the purpose of the certificate.
A company applying for a bank loan may need Companies Act definition. A SEBI-
listed entity needs LODR compliance. Choosing the wrong method and certifying
it is a professional risk.

Third: document handling. When a client sends their balance sheet, it often
contains CIN, PAN, GSTIN, Aadhaar-linked promoter details, mobile numbers,
email addresses. Forwarding that raw document to any external service — or
keeping it on a shared server longer than necessary — is a DPDP Act exposure.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. OBJECTIVES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Certify Genie was designed to achieve the following:

• Automate figure extraction from uploaded balance sheets (PDF and Excel),
  including scanned documents, without any manual data entry by the CA.

• Validate that the extracted figures are internally consistent — that Net Worth
  reconciles to the accounting equation, that the Balance Sheet balances, and
  that Working Capital arithmetic is correct — before the certificate is issued.

• Support all seven standard Net Worth calculation methods, recommend the
  appropriate one based on entity type, and embed the method label clearly on
  the certificate.

• Redact PII (CIN, PAN, GSTIN, Aadhaar, mobile numbers, email addresses) from
  documents before they reach the AI layer, achieving DPDP Act 2023 compliance
  at the workflow level.

• Generate output in both PDF and DOCX formats, with an ICAI-format letterhead,
  UDIN field, and consistent figures across the certificate body and all
  workings — eliminating the reconciliation step entirely.

• Give the CA full editorial control: override any figure, customise the
  narrative, rename row labels, set a PDF password, and preview the result in
  real time before generating.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. SOLUTION DESCRIPTION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The tool is a browser-based application that works in a five-step wizard. The
CA uploads a document, reviews what the AI extracted, enters their firm details,
selects the appropriate calculation method, edits the draft certificate on
screen, and downloads the final output. The AI does the reading; the CA does
the judgment.

At no point does the CA type a number from a document. The AI reads the document
and presents its reading — along with a confidence score and the exact source
line from which each figure was taken. The CA reviews, corrects if necessary
(overrides are available for every field), and proceeds.

The certificate output is a fully formatted document with the ICAI logo
letterhead, firm name and membership number, certificate number, certificate
date, client name, financial year, and a clean figures table with the Net Worth
computation. The last row is always automatically bold and highlighted green —
so the final figure never gets lost in the table. UDIN, if available, is printed
in bold below the signature block.

The first version of this tool was a single Python script that called Claude
with a prompt and dumped JSON to the terminal. That worked for text PDFs but
fell apart on scanned documents and had no validation whatsoever. The current
version automatically detects whether the PDF is text-native or scanned, switches
to vision mode for scanned documents, runs arithmetic validation with a 1%
tolerance, and supports seven Net Worth methods with entity-aware recommendations.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. ARCHITECTURE — MODULE BREAKDOWN
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[SCREENSHOT SUGGESTION: A high-level architecture diagram showing the 6 modules
below as connected blocks — Upload → Redaction → AI Extraction → Validation →
Method Selection → Certificate Generation — with the tech stack labels on each
block (PyMuPDF, Claude API, FastAPI, reportlab/python-docx).]

MODULE 1 — Document Upload & File Handling
  The CA uploads a PDF or Excel file (XLSX/XLS). Files are stored temporarily
  under a UUID-prefixed file_id. The system identifies the extension and routes
  accordingly. PDFs are first scanned for extractable text; if a page yields
  fewer than 50 characters of text, the system flags it as scanned and switches
  to vision mode for that document.

MODULE 2 — PII Redaction Pipeline
  Before any document content reaches Claude, it passes through a regex-based
  redaction engine built on PyMuPDF (fitz). The engine identifies and blacks out
  CIN numbers, PAN numbers, GSTIN, Aadhaar numbers, Indian mobile numbers, and
  email addresses. For text PDFs, matching tokens are replaced with
  stable placeholders (e.g., [PAN_REDACTED_7A3F1B]) before the text is sent to
  the API. For PDFs, redaction annotations are applied directly to the file using
  PyMuPDF's redaction workflow — the content is permanently overwritten, not just
  hidden. The CA also has the option to draw manual redaction boxes over any
  additional sensitive content before extraction runs.

MODULE 3 — AI Document Intelligence (Claude Vision API)
  Extracted or scanned page content is sent to Claude. For text-native documents
  the prompt includes the clean redacted text. For scanned documents, pages are
  rendered to base64-encoded PNG images (up to five pages) and sent via Claude's
  vision capability. Claude returns structured JSON: Share Capital, Reserves &
  Surplus, Accumulated Losses, Total Assets, Total Liabilities, Net Worth,
  Turnover, Net Profit, Current Assets, Current Liabilities, Working Capital,
  company name, financial year, currency, confidence score, source line for each
  figure, and prior-year comparatives where available. The system also handles
  two common extraction errors automatically: when Reserves & Surplus is already
  negative (accumulated deficit embedded), accumulated losses are nullified to
  prevent double-deduction; and when Claude returns Total Assets equal to Total
  Liabilities (a known Schedule III misreading), External Liabilities are
  recomputed as Total Assets minus Net Worth.

MODULE 4 — Arithmetic Validation Engine
  The extracted figures are validated against three standard accounting equations:
    — Net Worth = Share Capital + Reserves − Accumulated Losses (1% tolerance)
    — Total Assets = External Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity (1% tolerance)
    — Working Capital = Current Assets − Current Liabilities (1% tolerance)
  Each check passes, warns, or fails with the specific discrepancy amount shown.
  This gives the CA an immediate alert if the source document itself contains
  inconsistencies — or if the extraction missed a figure.

[SCREENSHOT SUGGESTION: The Validation Panel in the UI showing green checkmarks
for "Net Worth equation verified" and "Balance Sheet verified" with the extracted
figures beside each check. This is one of the most differentiated screens in
the tool.]

MODULE 5 — Net Worth Method Selector
  For Net Worth certificates, the CA selects the entity type (Company, LLP,
  Partnership, Proprietorship). The system computes the Net Worth figure under
  all applicable methods and recommends one:
    — Book Value: SC + R&S − Losses
    — Tangible Net Worth: Book Value − Intangible Assets − Goodwill
    — Companies Act [Sec 2(57)]: Paid-up Capital + Free Reserves − Losses −
                                  Deferred Expenditure
    — SEBI LODR: As per listed-entity norms
    — Net Asset Value (NAV): Total Assets − Total Liabilities
    — Adjusted Tangible Book Value: Tangible NW − Revaluation Reserve
    — Consolidated Net Worth: For group-level certification
  The selected method determines the row structure of the certificate table and
  the attribution note printed below the figures. This means two CAs using the
  same balance sheet but certifying for different purposes will each get the
  correct working without needing to edit the template manually.

MODULE 6 — Certificate Generation & Export
  The system generates both a PDF and DOCX. PDF is built using ReportLab with
  an ICAI logo letterhead, double navy rule, certificate title, client/FY info
  table, figures table, closing paragraph, and signature block. DOCX mirrors the
  layout using python-docx with identical formatting. Filenames are meaningful:
  CERT-2025-05-A3F1_CompanyName_FY2024-25_Net-Worth-Certificate.pdf — not
  random UUIDs. Optional PDF password protection encrypts the file before
  download. The CA can also email the certificate directly to the client from
  within the tool without downloading and re-attaching.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. INPUTS — WHAT THE CA NEEDS TO PROVIDE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

• The audited Balance Sheet as a PDF or Excel file (up to 10 MB for PDF,
  XLSX/XLS supported)
• CA firm name, CA name, ICAI membership number, and office address
• Certificate type: Net Worth / Turnover / Working Capital
• Entity type (for Net Worth): Company / LLP / Partnership / Proprietorship
• UDIN (optional, entered after generation if preferable)
• PDF password (optional)
• Purpose of Working Capital certificate (optional — appended to closing line)

No manual entry of financial figures is required. The CA reviews what the AI
extracted and overrides any figure that needs correction — but the starting
point is always the AI's reading of the document.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS FLOW — A REAL SCENARIO
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Scenario: A construction company needs a Net Worth Certificate to bid on a
PWD tender. The requirement specifies Net Worth computed as per Section 2(57)
of the Companies Act 2013. The client sends a scanned PDF of their FY 2024-25
audited balance sheet.

STEP 1 — Upload
The CA opens Certify Genie in a browser and clicks "Start Generating." On the
upload screen, the PDF is dropped in. The system detects it is a scanned document
(text extraction yields near-zero characters). A prompt appears: "Auto-redaction
has run — would you like to draw additional redaction boxes before analysis?"

[SCREENSHOT SUGGESTION: The file upload screen showing the drag-and-drop zone
and the "Add manual redaction?" prompt that appears after upload, with the
ShieldCheck icon and the two options: "Yes, let me redact" and "No, continue."]

STEP 2 — Redaction & AI Extraction
The CA clicks "No, continue." The system sends the page images to Claude Vision
with a structured extraction prompt. Claude returns JSON: Share Capital Rs. 25,00,000;
Reserves & Surplus Rs. 1,18,45,000; Accumulated Losses Nil; Total Assets
Rs. 2,80,62,000; Total Liabilities Rs. 1,37,17,000; Net Worth Rs. 1,43,45,000;
Financial Year 2024-25; Company Name extracted from the header.

STEP 3 — Review Extracted Data
The extraction panel shows each figure with its source line — "Share Capital:
25,00,000 — 'Equity Share Capital: 25,00,000'" — and a confidence score. The
validation panel shows: "Balance Sheet verified (Assets = External Liabilities +
Equity)" and "Net Worth equation verified." All green. The CA reviews the CA
details form: firm name, membership number, address.

STEP 4 — Method Selection
The CA selects entity type "Company." The system computes values under all
applicable methods and recommends Companies Act [Sec 2(57)] as appropriate for
a private limited company. The CA confirms this selection — it is exactly what
the tender requires. The computed Net Worth under this method is Rs. 1,43,45,000.

STEP 5 — Preview & Edit
The live preview pane renders the full certificate in the browser. On the left,
the editor panel shows the narrative, row labels, UDIN field, and PDF password
option. The CA types in the UDIN obtained from the ICAI portal, adds "For
submission to PWD Tender No. XYZ-2025" as additional notes, and the preview
updates in real time. The last table row — "Net Worth [Sec. 2(57)]
Rs. 1,43,45,000" — is bold and highlighted. The CA is satisfied.

[SCREENSHOT SUGGESTION: The split-screen Preview & Edit layout showing the editor
panel on the left and the live certificate preview on the right, with the ICAI
letterhead, double rule, Net Worth figures table, and signature block visible.]

STEP 6 — Generate & Download
The CA clicks "Finalise & Download." The system generates the PDF and DOCX
simultaneously. The download screen shows the certificate number (e.g.,
CERT-2025-05-A3F1), with one-click download for both formats. An "AI Extraction
Trail" panel at the bottom shows each figure and the exact source line it was
read from — a built-in working paper.

What used to take 45–60 minutes of reading, typing, cross-checking, and reformatting
took under three minutes. The figures are consistent. The method is correctly
cited. The UDIN is embedded. The working paper trail is built in.

8. TOOLS & TECHNOLOGY STACK
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Backend
  — Python 3.11+ with FastAPI (async endpoints)
  — PyMuPDF (fitz) — PDF text extraction, scanned page rendering, visual redaction
  — openpyxl / xlrd — Excel file extraction
  — Anthropic Python SDK — Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 (text + vision)
  — ReportLab — PDF generation (letterhead, tables, signature blocks)
  — python-docx — DOCX generation with matching layout
  — pypdf — PDF password encryption
  — Pydantic — request/response validation with strict type contracts

Frontend
  — React 18 + TypeScript + Vite
  — Framer Motion — animated step transitions and loading overlays
  — Tailwind CSS — utility-first styling
  — Lucide React — icon set
  — react-hot-toast — non-intrusive notification system

Compliance
  — PII redaction covers: CIN, PAN, GSTIN, Aadhaar, Mobile, Email
  — No document content or extracted data is persisted to a database
  — Session data is held in-memory per server process and cleared on reset
  — Aligns with DPDP Act 2023 data minimisation requirements

9. BENEFITS & IMPACT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Time reduction: A standard Net Worth Certificate that previously required
45–60 minutes of manual work (reading the document, typing figures, formatting,
cross-checking) now takes 2–4 minutes end-to-end. For a firm issuing 15–20
certificates a month, that is 8–12 hours of professional time reclaimed monthly.

Arithmetic accuracy: The validation engine catches equation mismatches before
the certificate is issued. In practice, these mismatches come from rounding
differences in the source document, prior-year adjustment entries, or simple
extraction errors. Having the system flag them forces a conscious review rather
than a silent error reaching the recipient.

Method compliance: Seven Net Worth calculation methods, entity-aware defaults,
and the method attribution printed on the certificate mean the CA is less likely
to issue a certificate using the wrong basis. This matters for bank submissions
(where the bank may specify the SEBI LODR method) and tender requirements
(where Companies Act definition is often specified explicitly).

PII discipline: Running redaction as a non-optional step in the workflow, rather
than relying on each staff member to remember it, makes compliance systematic.
The CA can see exactly what was redacted — CIN:1, PAN:2, GSTIN:1, and so on —
before the document is processed.

Audit trail: The AI Extraction Trail panel on the download screen shows every
figure alongside the exact source line in the document from which it was read.
This is a working paper by construction. For peer review or ICAI quality review
purposes, the CA has documented evidence of how each figure in the certificate
was arrived at.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
10. CHALLENGES & LIMITATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Scanned document quality: The vision pipeline works well on clear scans. Very
low-resolution photographs, documents with heavy watermarks, or handwritten
ledger pages reduce extraction accuracy significantly. The confidence score
reflects this, but the CA still needs to review the output carefully for
poor-quality inputs.

Complex Schedule III formats: Large companies with extensive notes to accounts,
multi-segment balance sheets, or consolidated financials with minority interest
adjustments can confuse the extraction. The system includes a correction for the
most common Claude error (returning Total Assets as Total Liabilities), but edge
cases in complex formats require manual override.

No persistent database: By design, this hackathon version uses in-memory session
storage. Certificates are saved to the browser's localStorage for history.
This is not suitable for a multi-user firm deployment without a proper database
layer and authentication.

Scoped to audited statements: The tool works from audited balance sheets and
financial statements. Provisional accounts, management accounts, or partially
filled formats will produce incomplete extractions. The CA needs to know this
and use the override capability for missing figures.

Excel support: XLSX/XLS extraction works well for structured trial balances and
formatted balance sheets. Unstructured Excel workbooks — with figures buried
across multiple sheets, pivot tables, or unusual layouts — are not reliably
parsed.

UDIN generation: The tool provides a UDIN field on the certificate but does not
integrate with the ICAI UDIN portal for generation. The CA must obtain the UDIN
separately from icaiudin.org and paste it in. A direct API integration would
close this gap.

11. FUTURE SCOPE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

• UDIN Portal Integration — Direct API connection to icaiudin.org to generate
  and embed the UDIN without leaving the tool.

• Multi-document ingestion — Allow the CA to upload the full set of financial
  statements (Balance Sheet, P&L, Cash Flow, Schedules) and let the AI build a
  consolidated extraction from across the set, reducing gaps from single-document
  uploads.

• ICAI UDIN register linkage — Log certificate number and UDIN against the
  client PAN/CIN to build an internal register, enabling firm-level tracking and
  ICAI quality review readiness.

• Working paper generation — Auto-generate a structured working paper document
  alongside the certificate, suitable for the CA's file, documenting the source
  document, extraction method, AI confidence, validation results, and any manual
  overrides made.

• Authentication & multi-user firm deployment — Add Google OAuth / ICAI SSO, a
  PostgreSQL backend, and per-CA certificate history so the entire firm works
  from a single instance with proper access controls.

• Direct bank/portal submission — Explore API integrations with bank portals
  (e.g., GeM vendor portal, PSB loan application systems) to submit certificates
  directly from the tool once generated and signed.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12. CONCLUSION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The ICAI has consistently emphasised that technology should enhance the CA's
role — not replace the judgment the CA applies, but remove the mechanical work
that surrounds it. Certify Genie is an attempt to apply that principle to one
specific, high-frequency task: the certificate. The arithmetic, the extraction,
the formatting, the cross-checking, the PII handling — all of that can and
should be automated. What remains is the CA's review, the CA's professional
judgment about which method applies and whether the figures are reasonable, and
the CA's signature. That is exactly where professional accountability should sit.
I built this because I needed it in my own practice. The code is running and
generating real certificates today. The path from here — UDIN integration,
working paper automation, firm-wide deployment — is clear. The foundation is
in place.
